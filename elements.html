What is Diabetic Retinopathy?

The leading cause of blindess in diabetic adults.

What we know:

Diabetic retinopathy is a condition that develops due to metabolic changes in vascular endothelial retinal cells in response to the high glucose levels (hyperglycemia) present in diabetics. It causes damage to the retina, which when impaired can cause severe changes in vision, and often results in blindness. Thus, in those with diabetes, diabetic retinopathy remains the leading cause of blindness (Hammes, 2013).

What we don't know:

The hyperglycemic conditions present in those with diabetes is known to be the cause of the development of the condition, but the mechanisms by which this excess glucose affects the retina are not very well understood. What is known currently is that the hyperglycemia lowers the permeability of the blood retinal barrier which leads to leakage of fluids from blood vessels causing the conditions of the retina to be disturbed. Due to the high sensitivity of the retina, this often leads to complete loss of vision (Shin, 2014).
