What is diabetes and the eye?

Basics

The eye

The eye is a highly complex structure that works to focus light that enters it, convert it into a neural signal and send this signal to the brain for interpretation. The eyes consist of many internal structures, which help it accomplish such an essential function. One of these structures is the retina, which is a specialized, light sensitive lining of neural tissue at the back of the eye that is responsible for converting light into signals that can be interpreted by the brain (Runkle, 2015). Due to its sensitivity, the retina is supported by the blood retinal barrier, a highly vascular structure, which nourishes the retina. The blood retinal barrier is made up of retinal photoreceptors and contains vascular and epithelial layers, both which work to maintain the proper conditions for the retina to function.
