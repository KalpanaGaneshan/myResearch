My Project

My Project:

Based on current knowledge, the researcher is investigating the effect of up-regulation of microRNA 200-b on the development of diabetic retinopathy, as well as the levels of other cellular factors (such as VEGF) to determine if any one of them could be used as predict the development and prevent and slow down the progression of diabetic retinopathy. The researcher will measure the levels of these cellular factor by use of various ELISAs. The researcher's hypothesis is that the up-regulation of microRNA 200-b will decrease VEGF levels and reverse the levels of other factors, thus increasing cell viability and maintaining cell homeostasis. Also, the researcher will be measuring cellular stress and viability as a measure of how this affects the cell as a whole and ultimately the progression of the condition. The implications of finding such a pathway are huge. Inhibitors of any molecule involved in the pathway can be used to halt the progression of the condition-and ultimately save someone’s vision.

Future Plans:

I am planning to investigate the role of the nf-kb pathway in this process. nf-kb is a transcription factor, which, when increased, increases production of inflammatory cytokine proteins. Examples of these cytokine proteins include ICAM1,iNOS, and COX2 mRNA. In addition, superoxide is shown to posisbly play a role in this increase of production. These cytokine proteins affect the permeability of the endothelial cells, which is how they are able to regulate its permeability.
